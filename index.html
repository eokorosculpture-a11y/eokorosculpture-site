Statement

The work
I strip away the superfluous to reveal the essential truth of a gesture, a state of being, a moment of human connection.

Emmanuel Okoro is a London-based figurative bronze sculptor of Nigerian heritage, from the Delta Region. His practice bridges West African aesthetic tradition with contemporary, organic minimalism — clean lines, richly patinated surfaces, bold presence.

Working across intimate gallery editions and monumental public commissions, his recurring themes are identity, humanity, legacy and immortality.

Education BA (Hons) Fine Art Sculpture — Wimbledon College of Art, UAL Commission London 2012 Olympic Games — Borough of Haringey Collaboration Banksy, ‘The Drinker’, East London — 2003 Signature Verdigris green patina
